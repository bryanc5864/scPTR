# ISMB 2026 Abstract

**Track:** iRNA (Integrative RNA Biology)

## scPTR: Decomposing Post-Transcriptional Regulation at Single-Cell Resolution

Single-cell RNA sequencing jointly captures spliced and unspliced transcripts, yet current analytical frameworks treat these primarily as a window into transcriptional dynamics. mRNA degradation — orchestrated by RNA-binding proteins (RBPs), microRNAs, and cis-regulatory elements — remains a major but largely unexploited axis of gene regulation at single-cell resolution.

We introduce scPTR, a computational framework that estimates per-cell, per-gene mRNA degradation rates (gamma) from standard scRNA-seq data and elevates gamma to a first-class analytical quantity. Splicing rates are inferred via quantile regression on phase portraits, and gamma is derived from kinetic steady-state relationships after adaptive Gaussian-kernel smoothing over a k-nearest-neighbor graph. Gamma estimates correlate strongly with published mRNA half-lives (Spearman r = −0.81 in sci-fate metabolic-labeling data; −0.35 to −0.40 in 10x developmental datasets), and predicted microRNA targets are significantly enriched among high-gamma genes across 59% of 215 tested families (p = 4.7 × 10⁻⁶⁵). Clustering cells in gamma-space uncovers expression-invisible post-transcriptional states — subpopulations with distinct degradation programs undetectable by conventional transcriptomic analysis — in pancreatic endocrine and hippocampal cell types, enriched for functionally coherent pathways. A post-transcriptional velocity, defined as the neighbor-averaged gamma gradient, reveals that degradation-rate shifts precede expression changes for 63–78% of transition genes during differentiation (p < 10⁻⁵), implicating post-transcriptional rewiring as an early fate-commitment signal. Library-size-corrected network inference identifies RBP hubs whose essentiality is independently confirmed by DepMap CRISPR screens (p = 6.4 × 10⁻⁵). scPTR is available as a scanpy-compatible Python package.
